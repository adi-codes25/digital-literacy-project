UPI Safety Checklist (2026 Edition)

1.  The "PIN for Payments Only" Rule
    Always remember: You never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. 
    If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "claim a refund" or "collect a prize," it is a scam.
2.  Verify the Beneficiary Name
    Before hitting 'Send,' check the legal name that appears on the payment screen. 
    Scammers often use fake UPI IDs (e.g., `ref.paytm@okaxis`) to look official.
3.  Reject Random "Collect Requests"
    If your app pops up with a request to "Pay" or "Approve" from an unknown person, decline it immediately. 
    These are the digital equivalent of someone reaching for your wallet.
4.  Secure Your Lock Screen & App
    Use biometric locks (fingerprint or face ID) for both your phone and your UPI app. 
    This ensures that even if your phone is stolen, your bank account isn't accessible.
5.  Scan Only Trusted QR Codes
    Avoid scanning QR codes sent via WhatsApp or email. 
    Only scan physical codes at verified merchant locations or those generated within trusted official apps.
6.  Avoid Screen-Sharing During Transactions
    Never use your UPI app while on a call using apps like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or Zoom. 
    Fraudsters use these to see your PIN and OTP in real-time.
7.  Set Daily Transaction Limits
    In your banking or UPI app settings, set a daily cap on transaction amounts (e.g., ₹25,000). 
    This limits the potential damage if an account is ever compromised.
8.  Update and Audit Regularly
    Keep your UPI apps updated to the latest 2026 security patches
    Regularly check your transaction history for small "test" debits you don't recognize.

If You Are Scammed: The "Golden Hour"
Speed is everything. 
Reporting within the first 2 hours (the Golden Hour) significantly increases the chance of freezing the funds in the scammer's account.

Call 1930: Dial the National Cybercrime Helpline immediately.
Report Online: File a formal complaint at cybercrime.gov.in
Notify Your Bank: Use your bank’s official app or 24/7 helpline to block your UPI ID and report the fraudulent transaction ID.
